### The “Fuzzy” Edges

These are the current conceptual seams where the document is still exploratory, under-integrated, or dependent on generalities.

#### 1. Business/Tourism vs Ethical Data Science logic is not yet fully fused
This is the biggest unresolved issue.

At present, the document contains **two strong but only partially reconciled logics**:

##### Logic A: Modul’s visible strengths
- tourism
- business
- sustainability
- entrepreneurship
- marketing
- internationality

##### Logic B: SEID / CHI / BBS / AI ethics
- AI and society
- ethical data science
- mediated environments
- responsible AI pedagogy
- collaborative AI
- immersive blended learning

The document implies these can reinforce one another, but it has **not yet fully explained how**.

Still needed:

- how ethical data science strengthens tourism education
- how AI/society themes support business and sustainability branding
- how CHI meta-projects let business, tourism, and data ethics co-exist without forcing curricular sameness
- how Modul’s business-facing identity can host ethical-tech inquiry without appearing off-brand

#### 2. “Visibility” is stronger than “implementation”
The draft currently has better language for:

- why Modul needs more visibility
- why CHI/SEID may help

than for:

- what a first-year implementation actually looks like
- who does what
- what counts as success
- what low-risk pilot forms are plausible
- how faculty participation would be structured

#### 3. CHI as philosophy is stronger than CHI as Modul mechanism
The document now has rich material on CHI mission, vision, meta-projects, and public examples.  
But it still needs sharper specificity on:

- what a **CHI-aligned structure at Modul** would concretely be
- whether it is:
  - a hub
  - a pilot
  - a faculty network
  - a course-linked initiative
  - a public showcase structure
  - a collaboration layer across existing courses
- what institutional “lightweight adoption” might look like

#### 4. UNESCO is conceptually strong but locally under-anchored
The UNESCO meta-project is useful as an exemplar, but the current draft has not yet fully determined whether UNESCO is:

- central to the Modul case
- just one demonstrative meta-project
- a grant pathway
- a branding device
- or a future extension

Right now, UNESCO functions more as a **proof of concept** than as a fully justified immediate priority.

#### 5. The audience is slightly blurred
The current draft sometimes sounds like it is addressing:

- internal Modul leadership
- faculty collaborators
- external grant readers
- or CHI ecosystem partners

This is not fatal at the current stage, but it means the tone and emphasis sometimes drift between:
- institutional strategy memo
- concept note
- background research
- and proposal prewriting
