## Purpose

This document compares the two syllabus files currently in the repository and then sketches a provisional "After" syllabus based on the broader repo evidence.

- [SYLLABUS.md](./SYLLABUS.md) is the compact official syllabus snapshot.
- [SEID2364 Syllabus 2026S Modul.md](./SEID2364%20Syllabus%202026S%20Modul.md) is the fuller semester-facing syllabus record.
- [SYLLABUS_COVERAGE_COMPARISON_MATRIX.md](./SYLLABUS_COVERAGE_COMPARISON_MATRIX.md) is the week-by-week comparison matrix for topics, terminology, assignments, and readings.

## High-Level Comparison

### What is shared

- Same course identity, credits, instructor, and Spring 2026 framing.
- Same core ethical model: Balanced Blended Space, mediated responsibility, hybrid human-machine systems.
- Same broad learning outcomes and assessment proportions.
- Same 14-unit instructional arc.

### What differs

- The official syllabus is shorter, cleaner, and more reusable.
- The detailed syllabus is operational, with week-by-week readings, more policy prose, and more semester-specific wording.
- The detailed syllabus includes additional administrative and pedagogical detail that is useful for reconstructing what the course actually did.
- The official syllabus is better as a baseline; the detailed file is better as a delivery record.

## Provisional After-Draft: What the Reconciled Syllabus Should Do

This is not the final prose syllabus yet. It is the structural draft for the after-version.

### 1. Keep the reusable course shell

- Retain the course title, credits, and core ethical framing.
- Retain the six learning outcomes, but present them in a slightly more compact, template-neutral voice.
- Retain the 14-unit course arc as the organizing frame.

### 2. Replace semester snapshot language with reusable language

- Remove direct Spring 2026-only phrasing from the main course description.
- Move live contacts, semester dates, and institutional logistics into a separate semester handout if needed.
- Keep the AI policy, but strip out wording that depends on a specific cohort or semester reset.

### 3. Rebuild the weekly sequence around repo evidence

| Unit | After-Draft emphasis | Evidence in repo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Orientation, Moral Machine, baseline ethical reflection | Assignment 01 and foundational resources |
| 2 | Intelligence, agency, and responsibility | Foundational reading set and BBS framing |
| 3 | Ethical frameworks and comparative analysis | Assignment 04 |
| 4 | Describing ethical environments, data, and bias | Assignment 05 |
| 5 | Mediation within socio-technical systems | Assignment 06 |
| 6 | Institutions, power, and accountability | Assignment 07 |
| 7 | Human-machine collaboration and authorship | Assignment 08 |
| 8 | Surveillance, privacy, and trust | Assignment 08 and related policy/case materials |
| 9 | Communication and ethical risk | Assignment 09 |
| 10 | Applied synthesis and systems communication | Assignment 10 and Week 10 signal-tracking materials |
| 11 | Emerging technologies and uncertainty | Assignment 11 reset-era materials and week 11 resources |
| 12 | Personal ethical framework development | Assignment 12 materials |
| 13 | Novel-context stress testing | Assignment 13 materials |
| 14 | Final synthesis and presentation | Assignment 14 materials |

### 4. Make the assessment section cleaner

- Keep the 50/30/20 weighting.
- Preserve the distinction between applied projects, personal ethical statement, and participation/engagement.
- Move low-stakes implementation notes into a short appendix or semester guide.

### 5. Trim readings into a reusable structure

- Keep the core reading categories.
- Move the detailed week-by-week reading list into a companion resource document.
- Treat the syllabus as the stable structure, not the full reading archive.

## What the After-Syllabus Should Look Like

The final after-syllabus should read like a cleaned and reconciled course document:

- baseline syllabus structure preserved
- semester-specific details removed or minimized
- actual implemented topics reflected in the weekly arc
- late-semester restructure acknowledged, not hidden
- auxiliary materials referenced outside the core syllabus body
