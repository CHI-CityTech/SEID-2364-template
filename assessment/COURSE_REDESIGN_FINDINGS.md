# Course Redesign Findings (Spring 2026 Iteration)

Date: 2026-07-08
Scope: Consolidated findings from assessment artifacts, delivery evidence, and redesign discussion.

## Why This File Exists

The redesign findings were previously distributed across multiple files (matrix, comparison draft, tasks, notes). This document consolidates the key findings and proposed course-structure changes into one working reference.

## High-Confidence Findings

1. The delivered course sequence diverged from the original static syllabus framing in important ways.
2. Lecture-note PDFs plus assignment artifacts provide the most reliable evidence of what was actually delivered.
3. Assignment dependency and assignment calendar placement must be tracked separately.
4. The late-semester sequence shifted toward misinformation, strategic systems, and synthesis.
5. Week 10 functioned more as a resource package/activity hub than a single lecture artifact.
6. The method architecture is two-layered:
   - SRDMPA as recursive organizing/project-progress cycle.
   - EDOCA as transition-level ethical/BBS diagnostic.

## Proposed Structural Updates

1. Move game-theory framing earlier in the semester as a recurring analytic thread, not only a late-stage add-on.
2. Treat strategic interaction and incentive reasoning as a through-line across units (scaffolded depth over time).
3. Reorder and tighten the "big picture" progression so students encounter system framing earlier, then iterate.
4. Preserve BBS as a central mediation language across weeks rather than a contained module.
5. Keep EDOCA aligned to transition analysis where students are diagnosing capability, distortion, observability, control, and authority.
6. Keep SRDMPA visible as process-language for project progression and recursive refinement.

## Course-Architecture Direction

1. Template-facing syllabus remains concise, stable, and reusable.
2. Detailed historical evidence remains preserved in assessment and syllabus source artifacts.
3. Weekly resources should be normalized after syllabus revisions are locked.
4. Lecture naming, week labels, and assignment mapping should align with evidence and intended scaffolding.

## Immediate Integration Targets

1. Update SYLLABUS.md to explicitly encode:
   - SRDMPA as organizing cycle.
   - EDOCA as focused transition diagnostic.
   - Earlier game-theory/strategic-systems through-line.
2. Update assignment sequencing notes where scaffolding changed from original plan.
3. Refine resource bundles by week after sequencing decisions are finalized.

## Decision Status

- Confirmed and ready to integrate:
  - Two-layer method architecture (SRDMPA + EDOCA).
  - Evidence-first week mapping approach.
  - Separation of dependency vs calendar placement.
- Pending finalization:
  - Exact week placement for game-theory-first scaffolding.
  - Final "big picture" sequence wording in SYLLABUS.md.
  - Final public/private split for redesign process artifacts.
