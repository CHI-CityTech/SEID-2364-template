## Evaluation Criteria

### Focused Use of the Case Study

Strong work identifies a specific misinformation intervention strategy from the expanded COVID case study and keeps the analysis focused. Weak work discusses COVID misinformation in general without clearly identifying the professional or technical action being evaluated.

### Application of ACM Principles

Strong work selects relevant ACM principles and applies them directly to professional conduct in the case. Weak work summarizes the ACM Code without explaining how it guides action.

### Application of IEEE Principles

Strong work selects relevant IEEE principles or commitments and applies them directly to engineering or technical responsibility in the case. Weak work summarizes the IEEE Code without explaining how it guides action.

### Comparison of Ethical Frameworks

Strong work explains how ACM and IEEE differ in emphasis, scope, and professional orientation. Weak work treats the two codes as interchangeable.

### Reflection

Strong work explains which code provides more useful guidance for this case and why. Weak work gives a personal opinion without connecting it to the frameworks.

### Clarity and Organization

Strong work is clearly organized, specific, and grounded in sources. Weak work is vague, overly broad, or unsupported.
