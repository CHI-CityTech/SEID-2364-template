## Assignment Overview

For this assignment, you will select one case study from the provided **Lite Case Study packet** and begin developing a preliminary specification of the ethical system it describes. This assignment starts the final paper process. It is not the final paper, and it is not expected to be complete or final in every detail.

The purpose of this assignment is to help you define the case clearly before making an ethical argument. A strong final paper depends on first understanding the structure of the system: who can act, what each actor wants, what signals move through the system, what information is visible or hidden, what rules shape behavior, and where ethical instability emerges.

This submission should be treated as a **working draft**. You should use it to establish a structure that can be expanded, revised, and corrected as you move toward the final paper. It is acceptable for some sections to remain provisional, provided that you identify what is still missing or uncertain.

You should use game theory as a descriptive tool, not as a mathematical exercise. You are not expected to solve a game, calculate equilibrium formally, or produce a complete simulation. Instead, you are using game-theoretic concepts to clarify strategic interaction, incentives, information limits, and conflicting goals. You should also use BBS and EDOCA to analyze mediation pathways, observability, control, authority, effort, and distortion.
