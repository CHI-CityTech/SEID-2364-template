## 1. Purpose of This NotebookLM Workspace

This NotebookLM workspace is intended to support the creation of a short explanatory video about the **COVID-19 misinformation moderation case study** used in SEID 2364.

The goal is to help NotebookLM:
- summarize the case clearly,
- identify the main actors and interventions,
- explain why the case is ethically ambiguous,
- and generate a concise video script or narrative suitable for students.

This workspace should help produce a video that is clear, neutral, and classroom-appropriate.

---

## 2. Core Case Study

The case study examines how social media platforms responded to COVID-19 misinformation during the pandemic.

The main interventions included:
- labeling misinformation,
- downranking or reducing visibility,
- removing posts,
- redirecting users to authoritative sources,
- suspending repeat violators,
- using automated moderation systems,
- and combining machine review with human review.

The case is not about whether misinformation existed. It is about how systems responded to it and what ethical questions those responses raised.

---

## 3. What NotebookLM Should Explain

The video should explain the case in simple terms:

- COVID misinformation spread rapidly online during the pandemic.
- Platforms used moderation tools to respond.
- Different actors were involved, including platforms, moderators, automated systems, public-health authorities, fact-checkers, users, and governments.
- These responses were not uniform across regions.
- The same technical platform could operate differently depending on the governance context.
- The case raises questions about authority, control, observability, transparency, public trust, and responsibility.

---

## 4. Key Ethical Questions

NotebookLM should help the video explain why the case is ethically complicated:

- Who should define misinformation?
- Who has authority to act?
- Who controls the moderation system?
- Who can observe what happened?
- How should systems respond when scientific knowledge is still developing?
- How should different platforms or governments shape the response?
- How should we balance harm reduction against free expression and trust?

The video should not try to settle these questions. It should present them clearly.

---

## 5. Regional Governance Contexts

If relevant, NotebookLM may briefly mention that platform responses differed across governance settings:

- **United States**: platform moderation often involved public-health communication and pressure from government officials.
- **European Union**: responses emphasized transparency, monitoring, and platform accountability.
- **China**: information control followed a more centralized model of state authority and public-order management.

These examples should be used only to show that governance context changes how moderation is understood and carried out.

---

## 6. Intended Audience

The intended audience is undergraduate students in SEID 2364.

Assume the audience:
- understands basic ethics vocabulary,
- may not know the details of platform governance,
- and needs a clear, concise explanation of the case.

Avoid overly technical language unless it is necessary and defined.

---

## 7. Tone and Style

The video should be:
- neutral,
- concise,
- explanatory,
- and classroom-friendly.

It should not:
- argue that one side is obviously correct,
- turn into a political debate,
- or overload the audience with too much detail.

The tone should be balanced and careful.

---

## 8. Suggested Video Structure

NotebookLM should aim for a short video with this structure:

### Opening
Introduce the case:
- COVID misinformation circulated widely online during the pandemic.
- Platforms responded with moderation tools.
- The case is ethically ambiguous.

### Middle
Explain the main moving parts:
- platform interventions,
- public-health involvement,
- human and automated moderation,
- and different governance contexts.

### Closing
Explain the ethical significance:
- the case raises questions about authority, responsibility, trust, and control.
- different responses may be justified in different ways.
- the case is useful because it shows how social, technical, and institutional systems interact.

---

## 9. What NotebookLM Should Focus On

The most important elements to surface are:
- moderation strategy,
- actors involved,
- governance context,
- ethical ambiguity,
- and distribution of responsibility.

NotebookLM should prioritize clarity over completeness.

---

## 10. What NotebookLM Should Avoid

Do not:
- treat the case as a general history of COVID,
- turn it into a partisan argument,
- overemphasize legal detail,
- or try to prove a single moral conclusion.

This is a case study for ethical analysis, not a policy verdict.
