# SEID 2364 – Ethical Journal Framework

## Purpose

Ethical reasoning develops through iteration, tension, uncertainty, and refinement. Not every thought is ready for publication.

This course uses a **dual-layer model** to support both intellectual risk-taking and professional accountability.

---

# I. The Private Ethical Journal  
*(Developmental Space)*

The Ethical Journal is a **private working document**.

It is a space for:

- Stream-of-consciousness provocations  
- Initial reactions to case studies  
- Intellectual uncertainty  
- Emotional responses  
- Draft definitions  
- Tensions and contradictions  
- Questions you cannot yet resolve  

The journal is not a performance space. It is a laboratory.

You are encouraged to:

- Take risks  
- Change your mind  
- Record disagreements with readings  
- Explore ideas that may later be rejected  

The purpose is development, not polish.

### Structural Rules for the Private Journal

1. **Chronological**  
   Entries should be recorded in order. Do not reorganize retroactively.

2. **Cumulative (No Deletions)**  
   Do not delete prior entries. If your thinking changes, add a new dated entry reflecting that change.

3. **Dated Entries**  
   Each entry should include the date. Ethical development is temporal.

4. **Unedited Record of Thought**  
   Do not rewrite earlier entries to improve clarity. Growth is visible through contrast.

5. **Private by Default**  
   You will not be required to share the contents of your journal. It is a protected intellectual space.

6. **Reflective Revisions**  
   If you revise a position, explicitly state what changed and why.

7. **Link to Course Moments**  
   When possible, note which slide, case study, or reading triggered the entry. This helps trace intellectual causality.

---

# II. The Public GitHub Repository  
*(Scholarly Space)*

Your GitHub folder functions as your **structured academic portfolio**.

This is where you publish:

- Refined definitions  
- Structured analyses  
- Selected reflections  
- Formal arguments  
- Your evolving personal ethical framework  
- Explicit standards or rulesets  

Material placed here represents ideas you are prepared to articulate clearly and stand behind publicly.

---

# III. Movement Between Spaces

You decide what moves from your private journal to your public repository.

The movement itself is meaningful.

It represents:

Internal reasoning → External articulation → Public accountability

Not all journal content needs to become public.  
But your public repository should reflect intellectual growth over time.

---

# IV. Intellectual Responsibility

Ethical maturity includes:

- Recognizing when a thought is exploratory  
- Recognizing when a position is defensible  
- Distinguishing reaction from reasoned judgment  
- Taking responsibility for public claims  

This course will guide you from exploration toward structured ethical articulation.

---

# V. Relationship to Final Ethical Framework

By the end of the semester, your repository should contain:

- A clearly stated personal ethical framework  
- Defined standards or evaluative principles  
- Explicit reasoning for those standards  
- Evidence of revision over time  

Your private journal supports this development.  
Your public repository demonstrates it.

---

# Summary

Private Journal → Exploration  
Public Repository → Articulation  
Final Framework → Accountability  

This structure mirrors the course’s broader arc:

Intelligence → Agency → Responsibility → Accountability
