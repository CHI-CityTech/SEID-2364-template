# SEID 2364 - Final Class Preparation
## Presentation Prep: Personal and Professional Ethical Frameworks

For our final class, please come prepared to present a brief, shareable version of what you consider the most important parts of your ethical position materials.

This is not your full Assignment 13 submission. Think of it as a public-facing snapshot.

During class, your positions will be stress-tested against a set of future (near and far) technology possibilities.

## In-Class Format

- We will use one shared Google Slides deck.
- You do not have access to the shared deck yet; access will be provided in class.
- Each student will have two slides.
- You should be ready to give a brief presentation of Slide 1 (about 2-3 minutes).
- We will leave a short setup window at the start of class for final slide adjustments if needed.
- Please edit only your own slides in the shared deck.

## Before Class: Complete Slide 1

Title: **Ethical Framework Snapshot**

Include the following:

- Your name
- One central personal ethical principle (from your Personal Ethical Position)
- Two professional ethical commitments (from your Professional Ethical Position)
- One professional boundary: something you would not build, support, deploy, or approve without further ethical review
- One unresolved tension that affects ethical reasoning or professional practice

### Important framing for Slide 1

- Keep this professional-facing and concise.
- Slide 1 is a snapshot, not a summary of the full assignment.
- Slide 1 is provisional and is expected to be stress-tested and possibly revised in class.
- BRIEF is the core requirement: your content must fit cleanly on one slide and be easy to read at a glance.
- Suggested ways to keep it brief: use short bullet fragments, avoid paragraphs, and keep each bullet to one short line.
- If content is too long, reduce wording rather than shrinking text.
- State positions as decision rules rather than broad value words.
- Example of stronger phrasing: "I will disclose meaningful AI use" is stronger than "transparency."
- Do not overload the slide visually. This is a presentation artifact, not a document.
- If you use terms like fairness, transparency, or accountability, briefly indicate what they mean in practice.
- You do not need to disclose private or personal details.
