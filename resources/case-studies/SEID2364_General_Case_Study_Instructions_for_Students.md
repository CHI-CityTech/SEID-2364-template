# SEID 2364 General Case Study Instructions for Students
## Applies to All Case Study Assignments

## Purpose
These instructions define the standard method for analyzing any SEID 2364 case study, whether the case is assigned by the instructor or selected by you.

Use this document as your default workflow unless a specific assignment explicitly overrides a requirement.

## What You Are Expected to Do
You are not expected to repeat the case narrative. You are expected to analyze the case as a mediated socio-technical system and justify your conclusions with evidence and ethical reasoning.

Your work should show how decisions, harms, and responsibilities are distributed across people, institutions, technical components, and governance structures.

## Required Analysis Steps
Complete all steps below for every case assignment.

1. Define the case focus:
- State the specific intervention, decision point, or system behavior you are analyzing.
- Keep scope narrow enough for rigorous analysis.

2. Build a mediation pathway:
- Map source -> transformation -> decision/output -> affected destinations.
- Mark where control, authority, and observability are concentrated or missing.

3. Identify stakeholders:
- Include direct participants, economic/institutional actors, governance/oversight actors, and design/accountability actors.
- Explain why each stakeholder is included.

4. Build a bidirectional risk and harm model:
- Analyze harms if intervention/system is implemented.
- Analyze harms if intervention/system is not implemented.
- Identify tradeoff harms that can appear in both directions.

5. Apply ethical/professional frameworks:
- Apply at least two frameworks (for example: ACM, IEEE, BBS, EDOCA, consequentialism, deontology, virtue ethics).
- Compare where frameworks agree, differ, or leave ambiguity.

6. Produce a governance recommendation:
- Propose a practical policy/process/technical response.
- Include accountability, transparency, contestability (appeals), and implementation considerations.

## Evidence and Source Requirements

### If the case is instructor-framed
You must cite:
- the assigned case file;
- all required frameworks/codes listed in the assignment;
- any additional required sources specified by the instructor.

### If the case is student-selected
You must provide:
- a short case-selection justification;
- at least 3 credible sources;
- at least 1 primary or official source;
- a brief source-quality note (reliability and limitations).

## Deliverable Expectations
Unless otherwise specified, submissions should include:

1. Case Focus
2. Mediation Pathway
3. Stakeholder Analysis
4. Bidirectional Risk/Harm Analysis
5. Framework Comparison
6. Governance Recommendation
7. Sources

### Expected Final Outcome for Lite Case Assignments
When an assignment uses a lite case packet, treat it as a starting scaffold rather than a finished analysis. By submission, your work must function as a complete case study analysis at the same quality level expected in the full standardized case studies.

## Quality Standards
Strong submissions do the following:
- Distinguish facts from interpretation.
- Make assumptions explicit.
- Address uncertainty and competing interpretations.
- Explain tradeoffs rather than forcing one-sided conclusions.
- Support claims with specific evidence.

Weak submissions usually:
- summarize the case without analysis;
- present only one side of harm;
- use frameworks as labels without application;
- make unsupported claims;
- ignore governance feasibility.

## Prohibited Shortcuts
Do not:
- rely on personal opinion without evidence;
- treat legal compliance as equivalent to ethical adequacy;
- copy pathway/stakeholder maps from case packets without adaptation;
- submit conclusions without showing how you reached them.

## Quick Checklist Before Submission
1. Did I clearly define my case focus?
2. Did I build a complete pathway with control/observability points?
3. Did I justify my stakeholder choices?
4. Did I analyze implementation, non-implementation, and tradeoff harms?
5. Did I apply at least two frameworks comparatively?
6. Did I provide a practical governance recommendation?
7. Are all required sources cited?
8. Can a reader follow my reasoning step by step?

## Note
If a specific assignment includes additional requirements, follow the assignment-specific instructions first and use this document as the common baseline.
