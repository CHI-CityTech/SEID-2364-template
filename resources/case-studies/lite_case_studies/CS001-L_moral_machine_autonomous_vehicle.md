# Case Study (Lite): Moral Machine and Autonomous Vehicle Ethics
## Decision Tradeoffs, Public Values, and Governance Boundaries

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
In Moral Machine-style autonomous vehicle dilemmas, systems face forced tradeoffs where harm cannot be fully avoided. Public response patterns often differ by context, culture, and perceived fairness. Real deployment settings add further complexity: sensor uncertainty, legal liability, safety engineering constraints, and institutional accountability.

The core challenge is to determine whether crowd preference data should inform vehicle decision rules, and if so, under what ethical and governance constraints.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders are directly exposed to harm in autonomous decision conflicts, and which stakeholders shape policy without direct exposure?
2. How does information move from scenario design to model policy to real-time vehicle behavior, and where do control and observability gaps emerge?
3. What are the harms of embedding population preference patterns directly into automated decision logic?
4. What are the harms of rejecting preference data entirely and relying only on manufacturer or regulatory rules?
5. What governance model best aligns safety, accountability, transparency, and public legitimacy in AV ethics decisions?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Mediation pathway map from scenario framing to operational vehicle decision outcomes.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with implementation, non-implementation, and tradeoff analysis.
3. Framework comparison memo using at least two ethics frameworks and one governance framework.
4. Policy recommendation with accountability assignment, audit mechanisms, and constraints disclosure.
