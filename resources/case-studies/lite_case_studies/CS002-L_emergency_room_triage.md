# Case Study (Lite): Emergency Room Triage During Resource Scarcity
## Allocation Ethics Under Institutional Constraints

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
A hospital emergency department has more critically ill patients than available ICU beds. Clinicians must allocate limited resources under uncertainty while balancing survival probabilities, urgency, and fairness constraints. Decisions affect not only patient outcomes but also family trust, staff moral injury, and institutional legitimacy.

The core challenge is how triage criteria should be designed, justified, and governed when no option avoids harm.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders bear immediate, delayed, and systemic harms under different triage protocols?
2. How do information quality, uncertainty, and institutional authority shape triage outcomes?
3. What harms occur under strict utilitarian allocation, and what harms occur under strict rights-based allocation?
4. What governance safeguards are needed to prevent hidden bias and preserve procedural legitimacy?
5. How should hospitals balance transparency with operational speed in acute triage conditions?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Pathway map from intake data to triage decision to downstream outcomes.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with bidirectional and tradeoff risk analysis.
3. Comparative ethics memo using at least two frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Triage governance recommendation with accountability roles, appeals logic, and feasibility constraints.

- Address both patient-level and system-level consequences.
