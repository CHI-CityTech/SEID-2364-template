# Case Study (Lite): Eminent Domain for Infrastructure Development
## Collective Benefit, Displacement, and Fair Compensation

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
A government acquires homes and businesses to build a highway or transit corridor intended to improve mobility and economic outcomes. Property owners are compensated under legal rules, but relocation can disrupt livelihoods, social networks, and cultural ties to place. Benefits are often distributed broadly while harms are concentrated locally.

The key challenge is how to justify and govern forced displacement when aggregate public benefit is uncertain or uneven.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders bear concentrated harms and which receive diffuse benefits under this policy?
2. How do decision pathways from planning models to acquisition action affect transparency and contestability?
3. What harms arise under rapid project approval with limited local participation?
4. What harms arise when infrastructure projects are delayed or abandoned due to procedural barriers?
5. What governance safeguards are required for fairness, compensation adequacy, and long-term accountability?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Pathway map from project justification to property acquisition to post-displacement outcomes.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with bidirectional and tradeoff risk analysis.
3. Framework comparison memo using at least two ethics frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Governance recommendation with compensation, appeals, and participation mechanisms.

- Distinguish legal process compliance from distributive fairness outcomes.
- Address both project-level and community-level time horizons.
