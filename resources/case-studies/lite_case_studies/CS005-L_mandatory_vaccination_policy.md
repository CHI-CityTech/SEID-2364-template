# Case Study (Lite): Mandatory Vaccination for Public School Attendance
## Public Safety, Autonomy, and Policy Legitimacy

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
A city requires vaccination for public-school attendance with limited exemptions. The policy aims to reduce outbreak risk and protect vulnerable populations but is contested by families who emphasize autonomy and concerns about medical risk. Enforcement choices influence trust, compliance behavior, and educational access.

The central challenge is balancing collective safety obligations with individual rights and procedural fairness.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders are most protected by mandates, and which are most burdened by enforcement mechanisms?
2. How do information pathways (health guidance, media, institutions, communities) shape acceptance or rejection of policy?
3. What harms arise under strict mandate enforcement, and what harms arise under broad exemption regimes?
4. What governance architecture can preserve legitimacy while responding to changing epidemiological evidence?
5. How should uncertainty and evolving evidence be reflected in ethical policy design?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Mediation pathway map from policy design to enforcement to community outcomes.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with bidirectional and tradeoff risk analysis.
3. Ethics comparison memo using at least two frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Policy recommendation with accountability points, transparency standards, and revision triggers.
