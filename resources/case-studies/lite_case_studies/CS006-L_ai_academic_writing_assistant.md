# Case Study (Lite): AI Academic Writing Assistant
## Learning Support, Authorship Boundaries, and Institutional Governance

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
An institution permits an AI writing assistant for drafting, editing, and summarization support. Students and faculty disagree on acceptable use boundaries, attribution expectations, and whether tool use undermines learning outcomes. Detection methods are unreliable, while blanket bans may be impractical.

The central challenge is designing a governance model that supports learning while preserving authorship integrity and fair evaluation.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders gain educational value from writing assistants, and which stakeholders bear integrity or assessment risks?
2. How does information move from prompt to generated text to submission and grading, and where does accountability become unclear?
3. What harms arise under permissive AI use policies, and what harms arise under strict prohibition policies?
4. How do ethical frameworks evaluate authorship, transparency, and professional responsibility in this context?
5. What governance architecture can support learning goals while maintaining credible assessment standards?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Pathway map from tool interaction to academic evaluation outcome.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with bidirectional and tradeoff analysis.
3. Framework comparison memo using at least two ethics frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Governance recommendation with disclosure rules, assessment redesign, and enforcement boundaries.
