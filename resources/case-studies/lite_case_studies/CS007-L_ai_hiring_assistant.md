# Case Study (Lite): AI Hiring Assistant
## Screening Automation, Fairness Risk, and Accountability Design

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
An organization deploys an AI hiring assistant to parse applications, rank candidates, and recommend interview pools. The system uses historical hiring data, job-specific criteria, and model-generated scoring. Recruiters can override outputs, but practical reliance on rankings is high due to workflow pressure.

The central challenge is whether the system improves consistency and efficiency without reproducing bias or obscuring responsibility for exclusion outcomes.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders control hiring decisions in practice, and which stakeholders are most vulnerable to hidden exclusion?
2. How does data move from resume input to scoring to interview decisions, and where do observability gaps emerge?
3. What harms arise under heavy reliance on model ranking, and what harms arise if such tools are rejected entirely?
4. How do ACM and IEEE frameworks evaluate fairness, transparency, and human oversight in this context?
5. What governance controls are required for auditability, contestability, and corrective action?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Pathway map from applicant data to hiring outcome with intervention points.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with bidirectional and tradeoff analysis.
3. Framework comparison memo using at least two ethical frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Governance recommendation with validation, appeals, and accountability assignments.
