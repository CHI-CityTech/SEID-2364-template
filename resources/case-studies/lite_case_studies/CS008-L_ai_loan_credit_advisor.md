# Case Study (Lite): AI Loan and Credit Advisor
## Risk Scoring, Access to Finance, and Institutional Responsibility

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
A lender deploys an AI system to evaluate credit risk and recommend loan decisions. Inputs include financial records, repayment history, and behavioral indicators. Human officers can review recommendations, but high-volume processing encourages strong dependence on system outputs.

The key challenge is balancing risk management and operational efficiency against fairness, explainability, and equitable access to credit.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders benefit from automated credit scoring, and which groups may face systematic disadvantage?
2. How does information move from applicant data to score to final lending decision, and where does responsibility diffuse?
3. What harms emerge under aggressive automation, and what harms emerge if institutions avoid automated tools entirely?
4. How do professional and governance frameworks evaluate explainability, contestability, and discrimination risk?
5. What governance architecture can support both prudent lending and fair access?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Pathway map from applicant data capture to final credit outcome.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with implementation, non-implementation, and tradeoff analysis.
3. Framework comparison memo using at least two ethics frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Governance recommendation with documentation, appeals, and periodic audit controls.
