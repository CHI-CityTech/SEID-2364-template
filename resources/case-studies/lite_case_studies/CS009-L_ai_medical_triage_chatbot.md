# Case Study (Lite): AI Medical Triage Chatbot
## Symptom Mediation, Care Access, and Safety Accountability

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
A healthcare provider introduces an AI chatbot that classifies symptoms and recommends actions such as self-care, urgent care, or emergency treatment. The tool is intended to reduce wait times and support triage efficiency, but users vary in language proficiency, health literacy, and symptom-report quality.

The core challenge is whether automated triage can safely mediate care access without amplifying error, inequity, or delayed treatment harms.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders experience the highest risk from false negatives, false positives, or delayed escalation?
2. How does information move from patient input to model classification to healthcare action, and where are high-distortion transitions?
3. What harms arise under broad chatbot deployment, and what harms arise if such systems are excluded from triage pipelines?
4. How do professional ethics frameworks evaluate safety, transparency, and accountability for system limitations?
5. What governance controls are necessary for validation, oversight, and patient recourse?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Pathway map from patient symptom report to care outcome with safety checkpoints.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with bidirectional and tradeoff risk analysis.
3. Framework comparison memo using at least two ethics frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Governance recommendation with clinical oversight model, failure handling, and audit requirements.
