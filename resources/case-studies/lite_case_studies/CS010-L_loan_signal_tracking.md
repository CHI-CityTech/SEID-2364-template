# Case Study (Lite): Loan Signal Tracking Scenario
## Signal Transformation, Temporal Conditions, and Trust Degradation

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
In a signal-tracking activity, a loan application signal moves across a pathway from applicant concept to application form, AI scoring, lending officer review, and final decision, with optional appeal branching. At each transition, information can be transformed, delayed, compressed, or reinterpreted.

The key challenge is how temporal and structural distortions alter trust, fairness, and accountability across the pathway.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which transitions in the pathway produce the greatest distortion risk, and who can observe or contest those distortions?
2. How does temporal delay or persistence affect trustworthiness of signals at later decision points?
3. What harms arise when institutions prioritize throughput over traceability?
4. What harms arise when pathway controls become too rigid and reduce adaptability or recourse?
5. What governance design can preserve signal integrity, accountability, and equitable outcomes across branching pathways?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Detailed BBS pathway map including transitions, branch points, and temporal conditions.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix with implementation, non-implementation, and tradeoff analysis.
3. EDOCA-focused ethics memo linking pathway transitions to control, observability, and authority shifts.
4. Governance recommendation with monitoring checkpoints, recourse rules, and trust-maintenance mechanisms.
