# Case Study (Lite): COVID-19 Misinformation Moderation
## Final Project Version

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
During the COVID-19 pandemic, social platforms adopted interventions such as labeling, downranking, removal, redirection to official sources, and account sanctions. These actions were shaped by public-health guidance, platform policy teams, automated classifiers, human moderation, and legal-political context.

The key challenge is how authority, control, transparency, and accountability are distributed when interventions affect both harm reduction and expression conditions.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholder groups are most exposed to harm under aggressive moderation and under weak moderation, and why?
2. How does information move from claim creation to platform intervention and then to downstream social effects, and where do observability gaps emerge?
3. Which governance responsibilities should remain with platform firms, and which should be externalized to regulators or independent oversight bodies?
4. How do ACM and IEEE professional principles evaluate moderation design choices differently in this case?
5. What transparency and appeals architecture would be ethically defensible across jurisdictions with different legal norms?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Mediation pathway map from claim production to moderation outcomes, with intervention points and observability gaps.
2. Stakeholder and harms matrix including bidirectional analysis (implementation, non-implementation, and tradeoff risks).
3. Comparative ethics brief using at least two ethics frameworks and one governance framework.
4. Policy recommendation memo with explicit tradeoff disclosure, uncertainty treatment, and implementation priorities.
