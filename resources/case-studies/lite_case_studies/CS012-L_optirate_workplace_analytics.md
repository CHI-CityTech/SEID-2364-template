# Case Study (Lite): OptiRate Workplace Analytics
## Final Project Version

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
Synaptix Solutions deploys OptiRate, an AI-enabled workplace analytics platform used in remote and hybrid work settings. The system processes behavioral and workflow signals such as activity metrics, communication metadata, and timing patterns to support managerial decisions on productivity and burnout intervention.

The company reports efficiency gains and earlier identification of team strain. At the same time, employees report concerns about surveillance pressure, autonomy loss, and the possibility that analytics outputs shape performance and promotion outcomes even when managers retain formal discretion.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders are most empowered by workplace analytics, and which are most exposed to loss of autonomy or due-process protections?
2. How does data move from employee activity capture to managerial decision-making, and where do control and transparency break down?
3. What harms become likely under aggressive deployment of analytics, and what harms emerge if organizations prohibit these systems entirely?
4. How do ACM and IEEE professional codes differ when evaluating employee monitoring, fairness, and accountable system design?
5. What governance architecture would make this system ethically defensible while preserving legitimate organizational needs?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. One-page pathway diagram from signal capture to organizational outcomes, with role labels and control points.
2. Stakeholder and risk matrix including implementation, non-implementation, and bidirectional tradeoff risks.
3. Comparative ethics memo using at least two ethical frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Governance recommendation with accountability points, appeal mechanisms, transparency rules, and short implementation plan.
