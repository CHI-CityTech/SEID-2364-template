# Case Study (Lite): Quantum Musico and Meta-Mozart
## Final Project Version

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
Quantum Musico explores AI music generation using a mixed corpus that may include public-domain classical material, machine-generated outputs, and contributions from living creators. A recursive training dynamic is possible when generated outputs are reintroduced into future model iterations.

The central challenge is how creative innovation, cultural stewardship, attribution integrity, consent obligations, and access governance should be balanced in a human-AI co-creation system.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholder groups gain value from recursive music generation, and which groups face highest risks around consent, attribution, or exclusion?
2. How does training data move through the system over repeated iterations, and where does provenance become difficult to verify?
3. Which governance controls are necessary for consent, withdrawal rights, attribution, and public access in this case?
4. How do professional codes and cultural stewardship ethics diverge when evaluating legal reuse of public-domain material?
5. What harms emerge from aggressive model expansion, and what harms emerge from strict limits that reduce experimentation and access?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. BBS pathway diagram with feedback loops, role transitions, and accountability checkpoints.
2. Stakeholder map with authority, control, and observability notes.
3. Bidirectional harms matrix with implementation, non-implementation, and tradeoff analysis plus uncertainty annotations.
4. Governance blueprint with policy commitments, technical controls, oversight process, and auditability requirements.
