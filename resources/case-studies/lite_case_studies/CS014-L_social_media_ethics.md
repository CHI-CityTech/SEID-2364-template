# Case Study (Lite): Ethical Distance and Virtual Locality in Social Media
## Final Project Version

This lite case provides shared factual grounding while leaving core ethical and governance analysis to you. For universal expectations on final deliverable depth, see `../SEID2364_General_Case_Study_Instructions_for_Students.md`.

## Scenario
Social-media communication moves across nested environments such as channels, groups, platform feeds, and cross-platform repost networks. As content travels, context can shift, visibility can be algorithmically transformed, and harm can emerge in audiences far removed from the original interaction setting.

The key problem is how to assign responsibility when outcomes are co-produced by user behavior, moderation practice, platform design, and recommendation systems.

## Core Questions for Analysis
1. Which stakeholders control visibility and intervention decisions at each stage of cross-platform content circulation?
2. Where do major context shifts occur from origin post to downstream amplification, and how do those shifts alter potential harms?
3. What harms become likely under strict moderation regimes, and what harms become likely under weak or inconsistent intervention?
4. How do professional ethics frameworks evaluate responsibility when no single actor controls the full pathway?
5. What transparency, appeals, and accountability mechanisms are necessary for ethically defensible governance of nested virtual localities?

## Required Student Analysis Outputs
1. Pathway diagram from content origin to cross-platform exposure, including context-shift points and accountability gaps.
2. Stakeholder and risk matrix with role-specific harms and bidirectional analysis.
3. Comparative ethics memo using at least two ethical frameworks and one governance lens.
4. Governance recommendation with implementation priorities, uncertainty treatment, and explicit tradeoff rationale.
