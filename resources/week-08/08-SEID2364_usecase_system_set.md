# SEID 2364 – Assignment 08
## System Selection Set: AI-Mediated Systems

---

## Purpose

This document provides a curated set of systems for Assignment 08. Each system is intentionally designed to support multiple deployments across different contexts, allowing for variation in risk classification, mediation structure, and ethical implications.

Students should select **one system only** and use it consistently across all parts of the assignment.

**If your prior system (from Assignments 4–7) closely resembles one of the options below, you must select a different system for this assignment.** Assignment 8 is intended as a fresh analytical start — do not carry forward a system you have already analyzed in depth.

These descriptions define the **core technical function** of each system. Deployment context should be introduced by the student in the assignment itself.

---

## 1. AI Hiring Assistant

This system processes applicant materials (e.g., resumes, cover letters, assessments) and produces outputs such as rankings, scores, or recommendations.

Typical capabilities may include:
- extracting structured data from unstructured documents
- matching candidates to job criteria
- predicting candidate success or fit

The system may operate:
- as a screening tool
- as a recommendation system
- or as a decision-support mechanism

Primary data sources:
- applicant-submitted materials
- historical hiring data
- organizational criteria or scoring models

Key mediation features:
- transforms human identity into structured evaluative signals
- introduces abstraction between applicant and decision-maker

---

## 2. AI Loan / Credit Advisor

This system evaluates financial information and produces outputs related to creditworthiness, loan eligibility, or financial recommendations.

Typical capabilities may include:
- calculating risk scores
- recommending loan terms
- predicting likelihood of repayment

The system may operate:
- as an internal banking tool
- as a customer-facing advisory interface
- or as an automated decision component

Primary data sources:
- applicant financial data
- credit history databases
- behavioral or transactional data

Key mediation features:
- translates personal financial behavior into institutional risk metrics
- mediates access to financial resources

---

## 3. AI Medical Triage Chatbot

This system processes user-reported symptoms and produces recommendations regarding medical care.

Typical capabilities may include:
- symptom classification
- risk estimation
- recommending next steps (e.g., self-care, urgent care, emergency services)

The system may operate:
- as a consumer-facing chatbot
- as a clinical intake support tool
- or as part of a healthcare infrastructure

Primary data sources:
- user-provided symptom descriptions
- medical knowledge bases
- clinical guidelines

Key mediation features:
- translates subjective human experience into structured medical categories
- mediates access to healthcare pathways

---

## 4. AI Academic Writing Assistant

This system assists users in generating, revising, or analyzing written content.

Typical capabilities may include:
- generating text based on prompts
- editing for clarity, tone, or correctness
- summarizing or restructuring content

The system may operate:
- as a general-purpose writing tool
- as an educational support system
- or as an integrated feature within academic workflows

Primary data sources:
- user prompts and drafts
- large-scale training corpora

Key mediation features:
- mediates authorship and expression
- transforms intent into language through probabilistic generation

---

## 5. AI Content Moderation System

This system evaluates user-generated content and produces decisions or recommendations regarding its visibility, removal, or classification.

Typical capabilities may include:
- detecting harmful or prohibited content
- flagging or removing content
- prioritizing content for human review

The system may operate:
- as a fully automated moderation system
- as a human-in-the-loop filtering tool
- or as a recommendation system for moderators

Primary data sources:
- user-generated content (text, image, video)
- moderation policies
- labeled training datasets

Key mediation features:
- mediates visibility and participation in digital spaces
- transforms expression into policy-relevant categories

---

## 6. AI Personal Finance Advisor

This system analyzes financial behavior and produces recommendations related to spending, saving, or investment.

Typical capabilities may include:
- budgeting recommendations
- investment suggestions
- financial risk profiling

The system may operate:
- as a personal assistant application
- as part of a financial services platform
- or as an embedded advisory tool

Primary data sources:
- user financial data
- market data
- behavioral patterns

Key mediation features:
- mediates long-term financial decision-making
- transforms behavioral data into prescriptive guidance

---

## Selection Guidance

When selecting a system, consider:

- whether the system can plausibly operate in multiple contexts
- whether decision authority can shift between human and AI
- whether the system can affect access, opportunity, or outcomes

The goal is not to choose the most complex system, but the one that allows for **clear variation in deployment and mediation structure**.

---

## Important Constraint

You must treat the selected system as a **core technical function**, not a fixed product.

All variation in the assignment should emerge from:
- how the system is used
- where it is placed in a mediation pathway
- who interacts with it

not from redefining the system itself.
