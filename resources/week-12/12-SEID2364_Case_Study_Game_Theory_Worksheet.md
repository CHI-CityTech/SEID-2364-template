### 3. Signals and Information
- What signals (data, messages, actions, cues) move through the system?
- Who generates, receives, or transforms these signals?
- What information is visible, hidden, or uncertain for each agent?

---

### 4. Rules and Constraints
- What formal or informal rules shape agent behavior?
- Are there meta-rules (who sets/enforces rules, how rules can change)?
- What incentives or penalties exist?

---

### 5. Strategic Interactions
- Where do agent goals align or conflict?
- Where does strategic behavior (cooperation, competition, deception, negotiation) occur?
- How might local success for one agent produce broader harm or ethical tension?

---

### 6. Ethical Conditions and Victory Conditions
- What does “winning” look like for each agent?
- What does an ethical outcome look like for the system as a whole?
- Where do these diverge?

---

### 7. Reflection
- What is the most important ethical risk or ambiguity in this system?
- How does this mapping help you clarify your own ethical framework?
