# SEID 2364 — Week 6 Framing Slides Outline

## Purpose

This deck sits before and around the BBS lecture content. It frames why the session matters, what students should notice, and how lecture concepts connect directly to Assignment 6.

Use this as a modular structure: keep core framing stable, then swap examples and pacing after first classroom run.

Based on [resources/Seid2364_Week04_Slides_Frameworks.pptx](../Seid2364_Week04_Slides_Frameworks.pptx) and [resources/SEID2364_Week05_Slides_BBS.pptx](../SEID2364_Week05_Slides_BBS.pptx):

## Recommended Flow (50-75 minutes)

1. Opening and session contract (5 min)
2. Why mediation pathways now (8 min)
3. From Assignment 5 to Assignment 6 bridge (7 min)
4. BBS core lecture block (20-30 min)
5. Guided pathway demonstration (10-15 min)
6. Assignment translation and expectations (8-10 min)
7. Exit prompts and next steps (3-5 min)

Adjust timing for shorter classes by compressing Sections 2 and 5.

## Slide-by-Slide Structure

## Section A — Opening Frame

### Slide 1. Title and Session Focus

- Week title
- One-line purpose: mapping mediation before ethical judgment
- Visual anchor (system map or blended space motif)

### Slide 2. Why This Session Matters

- Ethical analysis fails when pathway description is vague
- Complex systems distribute agency, authority, and access across layers
- Today builds method, not verdicts

### Slide 3. Learning Objectives (Student-Facing)

- Build explicit SVD pathways
- Map from a point of origin (observer perspective)
- Use distance symmetries (personal/local, remote, unreachable)
- Identify transformation and isomorphism breaks

## Section C — BBS Core Concepts (Bridge Into Main BBS Lecture)

### Slide 6. Relativistic Quad Premise

- BBS map is observer-dependent, not fixed
- Define point of origin first
- Local and distant are mediation-relative

### Slide 7. SVD as Operational Syntax

- Source -> Vector -> Destination
- SVD aligns with pathway tracing logic
- Same structure can be simple, chained, branching, or recursive

### Slide 8. Symmetries of Distance

- Personal/Local
- Remote
- Unreachable
- Distance is mediation-produced, not only geographic

### Slide 9. Dual Agency and Intelligence Symmetry

- Physical agency
- Virtual agency
- Cognitive and computational intelligence as analytic symmetry pair

### Slide 10. Isomorphism Break Prompt

- Where apparent symmetry fails
- Where AI is treated as tool vs collaborative partner
- Why this matters for accountability
