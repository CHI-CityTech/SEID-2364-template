# SEID 2364 — Week 6 Framing Slides Speaker Notes

This document converts the framing outline into a presentation-ready script with concise on-slide text, talk track, and transition cues.

Use with [Framing Slides Outline](./framing-slides-outline.md).

---

## Slide 1 — Title and Session Focus

On-slide text:

- Week 6: Mediation Pathways in Combinative Reality
- Goal: describe pathways before judging outcomes

Speaker notes:

Today we are building method discipline. We are not starting with verdicts. We are starting with pathway clarity so ethical analysis in later stages is grounded and reproducible.

Transition:

Before method, we need to establish why this matters now.

---

## Slide 2 — Why This Session Matters

On-slide text:

- Complex systems hide mediation
- Hidden mediation weakens ethics
- Description is prerequisite to judgment

Speaker notes:

Most ethical disagreement starts earlier than framework choice. It starts in incomplete system description. If we cannot trace where agency and access move, our ethical claims become impressionistic.

Transition:

So our learning target today is practical and structural.

---

## Slide 3 — Learning Objectives

On-slide text:

- Build explicit SVD pathways
- Map from point of origin
- Use distance symmetries
- Identify isomorphism breaks

Speaker notes:

Students should leave with one consistent pathway method they can apply to their Assignment 5 system. If they can map with consistency, they can evaluate with rigor.

Transition:

Let us anchor this in assignment continuity.

---

## Slide 4 — Assignment 5 to Assignment 6 Bridge

On-slide text:

- Assignment 5: system structure
- Assignment 6: system mediation travel
- Same system, deeper tracing

Speaker notes:

Assignment 6 is not a topic switch. It is a depth shift. Keep the same system and actors, then trace how information, authority, and responsibility travel through mediation.

Transition:

Here is where student descriptions usually start to fail.

---

## Slide 5 — Opening Dissections (Common Failure Modes)

On-slide text:

- Linear map for non-linear process
- Hidden vectors
- Agency confusion
- Authority-responsibility drift

Speaker notes:

Use this slide diagnostically. Ask students which failure mode appears in their own draft. This primes them to notice friction points before the formal BBS build.

Transition:

Now we introduce the BBS framing that resolves these failure modes.

---

## Slide 6 — Relativistic Quad Premise

On-slide text:

- Quad is observer-dependent
- Define point of origin first
- Local and distant are relative

Speaker notes:

There is no neutral map from nowhere. The first analytic move is selecting the primary actor perspective. Distance labels depend on mediation conditions from that origin.

Transition:

With perspective anchored, we can formalize pathways.

---

## Slide 7 — SVD as Operational Syntax

On-slide text:

- Source -> Vector -> Destination
- Core descriptive grammar
- Simple, chained, branching, recursive

Speaker notes:

Treat SVD as the minimum sentence of mediation. Some pathways are direct. Others branch, chain, or unfold recursively as we increase resolution.

Transition:

Once syntax is stable, we classify distance.

---

## Slide 8 — Symmetries of Distance

On-slide text:

- Personal/Local
- Remote
- Unreachable
- Mediation, not geography, defines distance

Speaker notes:

A nearby human can be ethically remote if mediation blocks response. A geographically distant actor can be local in effect through high-bandwidth mediation. This is the core reframing.

Transition:

Now we connect this with dual agency.

---

## Slide 9 — Dual Agency and Intelligence Symmetry

On-slide text:

- Physical agency
- Virtual agency
- Cognitive/computational symmetry lens

Speaker notes:

Students should identify where humans act physically and where systems act virtually. Then test symmetry claims: where structures align, and where they only appear to align.

Transition:

That leads to the critical diagnostic question.

---

## Slide 10 — Isomorphism Break Prompt

On-slide text:

- Where does symmetry fail?
- Tool role vs collaborative partner role
- Accountability implications

Speaker notes:

Require one explicit break analysis. The key is not rhetorical stance, but structural evidence: interpretation limits, consent asymmetry, explainability constraints, and responsibility gaps.

Transition:

Next, we demonstrate method with one pathway.

---

## Slide 11 — Dissection 1: Coarse Pathway

On-slide text:

- One high-level SVD line
- Source, vector, destination identified

Speaker notes:

Model a clean high-level pathway first. Keep it intentionally coarse so students can see what gets clarified and what remains hidden.

Transition:

Now unpack the vector to reveal hidden mediation.

---

## Slide 12 — Dissection 2: Vector Expansion

On-slide text:

- Expand vector into sub-steps
- Identify chain and branch points
- Mark recursive decomposition points

Speaker notes:

Demonstrate that one vector can contain multiple SVD relations. Show at least one branch and one chain. Emphasize that decomposition is optional and purpose-driven.

Transition:

Then we test map stability by changing observer position.

---

## Slide 13 — Dissection 3: Perspective Shift

On-slide text:

- Same pathway, new point of origin
- Distance categories recalculated
- Friction markers shift

Speaker notes:

Re-map from a second actor perspective. Students should see that pathway topology may persist while distance and ethical salience change.

Transition:

Now translate this directly into assignment requirements.

---

## Slide 14 — Assignment Part 2 Requirements

On-slide text:

- Full SVD line per pathway
- Source/destination node on relativistic quad
- Access and agency per stage

Speaker notes:

Stress completeness over length. The standard is traceability: another person should be able to reconstruct the same pathway logic.

Transition:

Part 3 adds explicit travel logic.

---

## Slide 15 — Assignment Part 3 Requirements

On-slide text:

- Declare point of origin
- Use local/remote/unreachable labels
- Mark physical-virtual transitions

Speaker notes:

Require students to justify why their chosen point of origin is analytically central. Then ensure distance labels are mediation-based, not only spatial.

Transition:

Part 4 is where structural friction becomes explicit.

---

## Slide 16 — Assignment Part 4 Requirements

On-slide text:

- Transformation and friction points
- Symmetry hold and isomorphism break
- Tool vs partner distinction

Speaker notes:

Ask for concrete evidence at each friction point. The strongest submissions identify where accountability and authority decouple as pathways progress.

Transition:

Here is the quality bar across all parts.

---

## Slide 17 — Quality Bar Snapshot

On-slide text:

- Complete pathways
- Reproducible logic
- Precise access mapping
- Visual-text coherence

Speaker notes:

Use this as the grading lens summary. If students know this bar before drafting, quality and consistency improve significantly.

Now show exactly how in-class work maps to submission.

Final logistics and forward link.
