## **AI Usage Statement – SEID 2364 Course Development (Spring 2026)**

### Overview

This course was developed and refined through extensive integration of multiple AI systems as both **tools and collaborative partners**. All original concepts, strategic decisions, learning objectives, and pedagogical choices remain the intellectual work of Dr. David B. Smith. AI systems functioned to enhance clarity, consistency, organization, and exploration of alternatives—not to generate foundational ideas.

### Tools and Collaborative Uses

**GitHub Copilot (Claude Haiku via VS Code)**  
*Primary Role:* Organizational consistency, editorial refinement, and documentation  
Used throughout the semester to:
- Review and improve assignment prompts for clarity and alignment with learning outcomes
- Organize GitHub repository structure and README documentation
- Ensure consistency in course material formatting, language, and tone
- Identify redundancies and overlaps in assignment sequences
- Refine section headings, introductions, and submission instructions
- Manage version control workflows and course material updates

*Impact:* Copilot functioned as an editorial and organizational partner, significantly accelerating the process of making course materials more legible and coherent. Critical decisions about content, structure, and pedagogy remained under my direct control.

**ChatGPT (GPT-4)**  
*Primary Role:* Brainstorming, lesson design generation, and iterative refinement

- Initial ideation and conceptual brainstorming for lesson sequences and assignment designs
- Generating multiple alternative framings of complex concepts (e.g., BBS, mediation pathways)

- Exploring different explanations and pedagogical approaches to core topics
- Testing clarity of learning outcomes and assignment rubrics

*Impact:* ChatGPT functioned as a thinking partner—generating possibilities that I then evaluated, modified, or rejected based on pedagogical intent and alignment with course goals.

### Authorship and Responsibility

All course materials remain under my intellectual and editorial supervision. No material was finalized without my direct review, modification, and approval. AI systems provided:
- **Enhancement of clarity** (not generation of ideas)
- **Identification of inconsistencies** (not determination of solutions)
- **Acceleration of formatting and organization** (not conceptual direction)
- **Exploration of alternatives** (not final decisions)

This mirrors the principles explored in SEID 2364 itself: AI functions most ethically when its role is transparent, bounded, and subject to human judgment and authority. The course was not *designed by AI*; it was *refined and organized with AI support*.

### Limitations and Ethical Considerations

- **Over-reliance on fluency:** AI-generated text is fluent but can obscure conceptual gaps. Careful editorial review was essential.
- **Assumption of consensus:** AI tends toward conventional framings. This required active pushback to maintain critical and nonconformist positions where appropriate.
- **Loss of authorial voice:** Early iterations required significant reworking to restore personal voice and specificity.
- **Unpredictability:** AI outputs varied across sessions, requiring iterative testing and validation.
- **Responsibility remains human:** While AI accelerated the work, responsibility for all course decisions—pedagogical, ethical, and institutional—remains entirely mine.

### Alignment with Course Values

This AI usage models the very principles students are asked to reflect on in Assignment 13:
- **Transparency:** All AI tools and their functions are documented here.
- **Bounded collaboration:** AI did not replace human judgment; it supported it.
- **Ethical clarity:** The purpose, scope, and limitations of AI use are explicit.
- **Reflective practice:** I have continuously evaluated whether AI use serves or undermines pedagogical goals.
